3. In Android Studio, open `app/java/com.example.<projectname>/MainActivity`, and update `appId`, `channelName` and `token` with the values for your temporary token.

4. Connect a physical Android device to your development device.

5. In Android Studio, click *Run app*. A moment later you see the project installed on your device.

    If this is the first time you run the project, grant microphone and camera access to your <Vpl k="CLIENT" />.

6. When the <Vpl k="CLIENT" /> starts, it does the following:

    * Sets the log file location, size, and logging level according to your preference.
    * Enables the dual-stream mode.
    * Sets the audio profile.
    * Sets the video profile.
    * Starts a network probe test.
    
    You see the result of the network probe test displayed in the network status icon.

7. Run the echo test.

    1. Press **Start Echo Test**. You see the local camera feed on the test device screen. 
    
    1. Speak into the device microphone. You hear the recorded audio after a short delay. 
    
        This test confirms that the user's hardware is working properly.

    1. Press **Stop Echo Test** to end the test before joining a channel.

<ProductWrapper product={["interactive-live-streaming", "broadcast-streaming"]}>
8. Select an option and click **Join** to start a session. When you join as a **Host**, the local video is published and played in the <Vpl k="CLIENT" />. When you join as **Audience**, the remote stream is subscribed and played.

9. After joining a channel, you receive toast messages informing you of some selected call statistics, including: 

    * The number of users in the channel
    * Packet loss rate
    * Remote video stats
    * Remote video state changes
