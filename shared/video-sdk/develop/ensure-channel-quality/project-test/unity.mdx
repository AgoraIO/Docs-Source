4. In **Unity Editor**, click **Play**. A moment later you see the <Vpl k="CLIENT" /> running on your development device.

5. When the <Vpl k="CLIENT" /> starts, it does the following:

    * Enables the dual-stream mode

    * Sets the audio profile 

    * Sets the video profile

    * Starts the network probe test.

    * Display the connected Audio/Video devices to your app.
    
    You see the result of the network probe test displayed in the network status label.

7. Test your audio/video devices:

    1. Select an audio and a video device from the dropdowns.
    1. Click **Start device testing**.
        You see a window opens and the camera feed from the selected video device is played in the window.
    1. To test the selected microphone, speak into the microphone.
        You see your voice is echoed every 500 milliseconds. This means your audio recording and playback devices are functioning properly.
    1. Click the same button to stop the test.
       

8. After joining a channel, you see messages in the debug console informing you of some selected call statistics, including: 

    * The number of users in the channel
    * Packet loss rate
    * Remote video stats
    * Remote video state changes 

9. You see the network status indicator updated periodically based on the result of the `OnNetworkQuality` callback.

10. Click **Low Video Quality**. You see the remote video switches from high-quality to low-quality. Click the same button again to switch back to hight-quality video.
