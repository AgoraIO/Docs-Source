1.  To run the app, execute the following commands in the terminal.

    - *Android*

        1. Enable the Developer options on your Android device, and then connect it to your computer using a USB cable.
        1. Run npx react-native run-android in the project root directory.

    - *iOS:*

        1. Open the ProjectName/ios/ProjectName.xcworkspace folder with Xcode.
        1. Connect your iOS device to your Mac using a USB cable.
        1. Click the Build and run button in Xcode.

1. When the <Vpl k="CLIENT" /> starts, it does the following:

    * Sets the audio profile 

    * Starts the network probe test.

    You see the result of the network probe test displayed in the network quality label. The console logs show all other metrics.

1. Press **Echo Test**

   Briefly speak into the microphone; your audio is played back to you after about 4 seconds. The playback means that the speakers and microphone on your device are working properly. The quality of the playback indicates the suitability of your network connection for audio communication. Press the same button to stop the echo test.

1. Press **Join** to connect to the same channel as your web demo.


1. After joining a channel, you see messages in the console informing you of some selected call statistics, including: 

    * The number of users in the channel
    * Packet loss rate

1. You see the network quality label updated periodically based on the result of the `onNetworkQuality` callback.
